Spectrum Therapeutics Navigation Re-design

Purpose

As a UX lead for optimizing Spectrum Therapeuticâ€™s website while working at Canopy Growth, the navigation and content were one of the first problems that needed to be solved to create a seamless experience for health professionals and patients. The plan was to add more educational content without over cluttering the current navigation. The second problem was because the navigation was first built using three different web services (AEM, Shopify and a portal) which resulted in a broken experience for logged in users.

Results

The initial idea was to merge all three into one web service, but this decision, later on,had to change due to budget and priority adjustments. I ended up changing it slightly to meet the new technical requirements and split in a three tabbed approach, whereas the original approach was to only keep Health Professionals and Patients separated with Shop merged in Patients.

I had to try to understand both health professionals and patients in the medical cannabis space. I asked for some preliminary research that was conducted a while back, and I took the initiative to plan and test both user groups to explore the website. I wanted to observe how different they are in navigating through the site while being asked particular task questions. We also identified through insights from Customer Care that there was a problem for existing customers that noticed the confusing changes between AEM and Shopify navigation that seem to be the same but don'tt have consistent login status.

The user research was done via UserTesting for flexibility and time efficiency as the project had a tight deadline along with other tasks I managed. As a reminder, during this time at Canopy Growth, we were only a team of three UX designers.

Usability testing insights

Here are the following key insights from testing with 14 participants (6 health professionals + 8 patients):

  • Only some of the health professionals said they would consider registering with Spectrum Therapeutics due to not feeling 100% confident about medical cannabis and how to introduce it in their practice
  • Most of the health professionals liked the Research page because it demonstrates that Spectrum has lots of knowledge of medical cannabis which invokes trustworthiness
  • A few patients thought the info on THC/CBD was too complex for them and not enough info on conditions/illnesses
  • A few were expecting The Spectrum page was going to be like the About Us page
  • Most explored the Patients and The Spectrum pages to learn about the company and medical cannabis
  • Some patients went directly to the Shop and attempted to learn more about the products and medical cannabis by trying to find their way back, noticing they were on a different website

Collaboration with stakeholders

The navigation has gone through several iterations, a few rounds before UserTesting to validate new ideas by analyzing the current state of the navigation, and then after testing to help guide changes to our initial ideas. I was working collaboratively with the product manager and Spectrum stakeholders. We also facilitated an in-person workshop with stakeholders to get a better understanding of the key health professional personas using preliminary research, what they know about health professionals in the medical cannabis space, as well as using the findings from the UserTesting. After I compiled the discussion points from the workshop into user journey maps, we finalized the proposed navigation (in a split between Patients and Health Professionals) and planned a phased approach to improving the content for health professionals.

As for the Patients side of the user journeys, I took some of the insights from the first round of testing and a second round of testing in parallel with another project that involved improving the content. I used the second round of testing to further improve the navigation from the patients' perspective.

As part of the process of re-designing the navigation, I created sitemaps and user flows to demonstrate the new structure more easily at a glance for stakeholders and developers to understand.


Overall, I lead the navigation re-design from discovery and planning to the interaction design and look and feel, while collaboratively working with stakeholders. At the final stages of the project, I was working closely with the front-end developers to ensure the vision was met. I also made sure that the Adobe Experience Manager (AEM) and Shop navigations were about 100% the same as they were developed by different developers.

The end result of the new navigation will not only support a more seamless experience for each user group but also support a clear journey to more educational content.
